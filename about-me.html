What is your background?

I was solving puzzles before I could even walk, the first puzzle my parents got me was a colorful shape sorting cube puzzle. That was the cube that had different shape perforations and the goal was to insert all the shapes into their appropriate cutouts until the cube was full. I had mastered that puzzle before I turned 1. I quickly moved on to more complex puzzles that would require analyzing and critical thinking. I also had a LARGE collection of legos, I didn’t necessarily follow the directions on the box. I would either try to mimic the structure from looking at the pictures on the box or I would create my own version of the structure I thought was better.

In school, I was one of the weird kids who actually loved math, specifically word problems. World problems made sense to me because I understood the concept necessary for problem solving: analyze the problem, pull out the pertinent information, solve the equation and then think about how the answer makes sense in the context of the problem.

After graduating high school I decided to study Architecture at the University of Illinois at Chicago because it had the best of both worlds, design and problem solving. My architectural design studios taught me how to receive critiques of my work and apply it to my projects to make them that much better. Upon graduating from the university, I began working at a renowned architecture firm in Chicago where I realized I excelled at problem solving as well as simplifying the end user’s life through my designs. It is here where I became increasingly interested in learning the skills required to become a UX/UI designer.
